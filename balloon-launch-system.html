Balloon Launch System (BLS)

a balloon assisted rocket-launch system

Advanced Telemetrics

BLS constantly samples data from barometric, temperature, and gyroscopic sensors as well as positioning systems to determine when to execute the launch command. With this automation, along with manual failsafes, BLS is both precise and reliable.

Reusability Is Key

Once BLS determines that it has reached the desired launch conditions, the flight computer and advanced sensors, safely housed in the instrument module, detach themselves from the launch platform and balloon array. As the insturment module deploys its parachute, the most expensive parts of BLS gently glide back to terra firma, aided by a parachute, to be reused in subsequent missions.

Give Your Payload A Lift

Balloon Launch System (a.k.a. BLS) is our answer to standard rocket launches. BLS is compact, efficient, and best of all, incredibly inexpensive. Due to its simplicty and reusability, BLS offers the possibility of orbital insertion at incredibly low costs.

BLS is comprised of three main parts. First is the balloon array which provides a smooth ride to the upper atmosphere. Next is the launch platform and rocket. Once BLS reaches the desired launch conditions, your payload will launch on top of a small rocket. Last is the instrument module. This is where the flight computer and sensors are stored. Once BLS executes the launch, the instrument module detaches and glides safely back to earth.
